Facts to learn about monkeys and beavers:

  • Several monkey species are threatened or endangered due to habitat loss, hunting, and the pet trade.
  • Beavers typically live in family units, consisting of a breeding pair and their offspring.
  • Young monkeys engage in playful behavior, which is essential for developing social skills and coordination.
  • Beavers build lodges with sticks and mud, which can be several feet tall and serve as a safe haven from predators.
  • Monkeys use a range of facial expressions to communicate emotions and social signals.
  • Beavers communicate through vocalizations, tail slaps, and scent markings to signal danger.
  • Monkeys are highly social animals and live in groups called troops, which can range from a few individuals to over 100.
  • Most monkeys are omnivorous, eating a diet that includes fruits, seeds, flowers, and small animals.
  • There are over 260 species of monkeys, divided into New World monkeys (found in the Americas) and Old World monkeys (found in Africa and Asia).
  • Beavers are herbivores, primarily eating tree bark, leaves, and aquatic plants.
  • Beavers can build dams that can be over a mile long, significantly altering their environment.
